Mental Health




What is Mental Health?

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act.

Types of mental illness:
  • Depression.
  • ADHD
  • mood disorders (such as depression or bipolar disorder)
  • anxiety disorders.
  • personality disorders.
  • psychotic disorders (such as schizophrenia)
  • eating disorders.
  • trauma-related disorders (such as "PTSD" post-traumatic stress disorder)
  • obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD)
  • substance abuse disorders
The five main warning signs of mental illness are as follows:
  • Excessive paranoia, worry, or anxiety.
  • Long-lasting sadness or irritability.
  • Extreme changes in moods.
  • Social withdrawal.
  • Dramatic changes in eating or sleeping pattern.
What causes mental health problems?
  • childhood abuse, trauma, or neglect.
  • social isolation or loneliness.
  • experiencing discrimination and stigma, including racism.
  • social disadvantage, poverty or debt.
  • bereavement (losing someone close to you)
  • severe or long-term stress.
  • having a long-term physical health condition.
Can mental health be cured?

Yes, treatement can involve botn medications and pyschotherapy, depending on the disease and severity. At this time, most mental health illnesses cannot be cured, but can usually be treated and minimize its symptoms.

How to be mentally healthy?
  • Talk about your feelings, it can help you stay in good mental health and deal with times when you feel troubled.
  • Ask for help, prefebly face to face
  • Take your time
  • Seek professional help
  • Accept who you are
  • Avoid over thinking
  • Eat a brain-healthy diet
  • Take a relaxation practice such as yoga, meditation and deep breathing
